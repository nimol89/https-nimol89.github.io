Project Goals

This project explores the interconnectedness of education, economic growth, and environmental management as key pillars for promoting sustainable development in Cambodia. While rapid economic expansion has fueled improvements in living standards, it has also led to environmental degradation and socio-economic disparities. Addressing these challenges requires a holistic strategy that integrates educational reform, sustainable economic policies, and robust environmental governance. By enhancing environmental education, empowering local communities, and fostering a green economy, this approach seeks to cultivate an informed populace that actively participates in sustainable practices. Through a comprehensive review of current policies and developmental strategies, we propose a multi-sectoral framework that aligns national educational initiatives with environmental and economic goals. This paper argues that investing in education and adopting sustainable practices are critical to balancing economic growth with ecological conservation, ensuring a resilient and sustainable future for Cambodia. .

Introduction

The emerging challenge of Cambodia lies critically in the uneven expanse of electricity consumption versus the income of the people in the local community as the country is known to have the highest price of electricity in ASEAN. Given the potential of being the chairman of ASEAN in 2022, another point of discussion arises upon the progress of renewable energy development in the country, which is significant to boost income growth and bring about modernization 4.0.
